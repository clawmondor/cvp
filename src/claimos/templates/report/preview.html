Methodology

The contents inventory was developed using the following process:

  1. Evidence intake. All available evidence — including photographs, video walkthroughs, receipts, prior appraisals, and policyholder-provided inventories — was collected and organized by room.
  2. Item identification. Each item was identified by description, brand (where visible or recalled), model (where available), and room location.
  3. Current market pricing. Replacement cost values (RCV) reflect the current retail price of a like-kind-and-quality replacement item, sourced from major U.S. retailers at the time of documentation. Each price is supported by a source URL and capture date recorded in the Pricing Audit Trail (Section 10).
  4. Depreciation. Actual cash values (ACV) are computed using straight-line depreciation per the schedule in Section 7. All computations are algorithmic and consistent across every item — no specialist discretion is applied to individual depreciation rates.
  5. Quality review. Each line item was individually reviewed and confirmed by a senior specialist before inclusion in this report.

Depreciation Methodology

Depreciation is computed using the straight-line method applied to each item's useful life, age, and condition. The formula is: 

dep_rate          = 1 / useful_life_years
accumulated_dep   = min(dep_rate × age_years × condition_multiplier, 1 − floor_pct)
acv_unit          = round(rcv_unit × (1 − accumulated_dep))
acv_total         = acv_unit × quantity

Condition multipliers: Excellent 0.75 · Above Average 0.90 · Average 1.00 · Below Average 1.15. Items with no assigned useful life (e.g., jewelry, artwork) are not depreciated — ACV equals RCV.
